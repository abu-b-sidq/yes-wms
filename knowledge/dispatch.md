# Dispatch and Shipping Procedures

## Objective
Dispatch turns picked inventory into confirmed outbound shipments with the right documentation, carrier handoff, and loading accuracy.

## Dispatch Principles
- Nothing should reach the truck unless it is fully verified and shipment-ready.
- Staging discipline is essential; unmarked carts and floor pallets create loading mistakes.
- Carrier cutoff times should shape wave release and final verification priorities.

## Standard Dispatch Process
1. Receive completed picked orders from picking or packing.
2. Consolidate all lines by shipment, route, or invoice reference.
3. Verify SKU count, carton count, pallet count, and any customer compliance labels.
4. Confirm shipment status, destination, and carrier assignment.
5. Move stock into the correct dispatch lane or route stage.
6. Sequence loads based on stop order, route logic, or vehicle loading constraints.
7. Load the vehicle while verifying each handling unit against the shipment.
8. Confirm final documents such as manifest, invoice, packing list, seal number, or proof-of-load.
9. Update dispatch completion promptly in system and hand over documents to transport.

## Dispatch Staging Rules
- Stage by route, carrier, departure time, or dock door.
- Separate same-day urgent shipments from standard waves.
- Do not mix partially verified and ready-to-load stock in the same lane.
- Use visible lane labels and departure boards to prevent last-minute searching.

## Loading Techniques
- Load last-drop stock first when route unloading follows reverse stop sequence.
- Keep fragile or crush-sensitive stock accessible and protected.
- Secure mixed loads to prevent transit damage.
- Keep temperature-sensitive shipments within controlled exposure limits.

## Pre-Ship Verification
- Carton count matches shipment record
- SKU or handling unit scan matches dispatch list
- Lot, expiry, or compliance documents included where required
- Customer labeling and routing labels applied correctly
- Vehicle cleanliness and suitability checked before loading

## Exception Handling

### Missing Shipment Piece
1. Search dispatch staging and the last pick or pack handoff point.
2. Review whether the handling unit was picked but not staged, or staged under the wrong route.
3. Hold vehicle release if the missing piece affects customer commitment or invoice accuracy.

### Wrong Carrier or Route Stage
1. Stop loading immediately.
2. Move the shipment to the correct dispatch lane with traceability.
3. Review visual controls if route mis-staging happens repeatedly.

### Carrier Delay
1. Protect loaded or staged inventory according to product needs.
2. Re-sequence dock activity to avoid blocking the next departure.
3. Update customer service or transport planning if cutoff risk appears.

### Load Rejection
1. Record the reason for rejection.
2. Move stock back to controlled dispatch staging or quarantine as required.
3. Preserve chain of custody and document any seal or condition issues.

## Dispatch KPIs
- On-time dispatch percentage
- Load accuracy
- Order cycle time from pick completion to vehicle departure
- Dispatch dwell time
- Carrier waiting time
- Missed cutoff count

## Consultant Improvement Techniques
- Use wave release tied to carrier cutoffs, not only order print timing.
- Measure staging dwell separately from picking productivity so outbound bottlenecks are visible.
- Use route-based staging maps during peak periods.
- Review late departures with a Pareto split across picking, packing, staging, transport, and documentation causes.
