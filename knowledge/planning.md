# Supply Chain Planning and Inventory Policy Guide

## Objective
Planning aligns supply, storage, labor, and service targets so the warehouse is not forced into constant reactive work.

## Planning Principles
- Service goals must be translated into inventory, labor, and replenishment policy.
- Variability matters as much as average demand.
- Short lead times and good supplier discipline reduce the need for excess stock.
- Warehouse congestion often starts with poor planning, not only poor execution.

## Core Planning Techniques

### Safety Stock Thinking
- Safety stock protects against demand spikes and lead-time variability.
- High-variability or critical items need more protection than stable, low-impact items.
- Review safety stock whenever service targets, lead times, or demand patterns change.

### Reorder Point Thinking
- Reorder point should cover expected demand during replenishment lead time plus safety stock.
- Long or unreliable supply lead times require earlier replenishment triggers.

### Min-Max Policy
- Easy to operate and suitable for many warehouse-managed items.
- Min should reflect reorder trigger; max should reflect practical replenishment batch and storage capacity.

### ABC-XYZ Policy Design
- Use value or velocity plus variability to choose replenishment discipline.
- AX items may need tight review and strong availability.
- CZ items may need lower stocking commitment and more cautious buying.

### Supplier Segmentation
- Strategic and unstable suppliers need more attention than stable commodity vendors.
- Track lead time reliability, MOQ, labeling compliance, and quality performance.

### Capacity Planning
- Plan dock, labor, and storage for peak days, not only average days.
- Check whether inbound peaks are creating downstream putaway and picking bottlenecks.

## S&OP and Cross-Functional Alignment
- Sales, procurement, operations, and warehouse teams should review forecast changes together.
- Promotions, launches, and seasonal peaks need pre-build and slotting plans.
- If a new demand event is announced late, expect manual work, overflow, and service risk.

## Planning Review Cadence
- Daily: priority shortages, urgent receipts, labor and cutoff risks
- Weekly: replenishment settings, supplier issues, slow-moving stock, capacity hot spots
- Monthly: ABC review, slotting refresh, supplier scorecard, obsolescence actions
- Quarterly: policy review for safety stock, service level, and network constraints

## Consultant Improvement Techniques
- Tie planning policy to service commitment by SKU family, not one global target.
- Review aged purchase commitments versus actual demand to reduce excess stock.
- Use scenario planning before promotions or customer onboarding.
- Connect supplier scorecards to real warehouse pain points such as relabeling, damages, and over-short receipts.
