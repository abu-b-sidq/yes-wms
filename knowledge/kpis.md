# Supply Chain KPIs and Root-Cause Playbook

## Objective
Use KPIs to expose operational problems early, then run disciplined root-cause routines that lead to corrective action instead of repeated firefighting.

## KPI Design Principles
- Measure service, cost, quality, and inventory together. One metric alone gives a distorted view.
- Separate leading indicators from lagging indicators.
- Track trends by process area and shift, not only facility total.
- Use a small number of trusted metrics before adding more dashboards.

## Core Warehouse and Supply Chain KPIs

### Service
- OTIF: on time, in full
- Order fill rate
- Dispatch on-time percentage
- Stockout rate

### Inbound
- Supplier OTIF
- Receipt accuracy
- Dock-to-stock time
- Damage-on-receipt rate

### Inventory
- Inventory accuracy
- First-count accuracy
- Adjustment rate
- Aged inventory percentage
- Days of inventory on hand, if policy uses it

### Outbound
- Pick accuracy
- Lines picked per hour
- Order cycle time
- Missed carrier cutoff count

### Productivity and Flow
- Labor hours per order or line
- Equipment utilization
- Queue or dwell time by zone
- Emergency replenishment rate

## Daily Management Routine
1. Review yesterday's service misses, safety incidents, and major variances.
2. Review today's inbound, outbound, labor, and capacity plan.
3. Highlight the top three risks to service.
4. Assign owners and due times for each action.
5. Follow up on open corrective actions from previous days.

## Root-Cause Techniques

### Pareto Analysis
- Start with the biggest repeat failures first.
- Example: top causes of short pick, late dispatch, or receiving claims.

### 5 Whys
- Use for one focused problem where the team can trace a chain of causes.
- Keep asking why until the answer points to a process, system, training, or design cause.

### Fishbone or Cause-and-Effect
- Good for broader issues spanning people, process, equipment, material, environment, and data.

### Control-Point Review
- Check where the process should have detected the problem earlier.
- If errors are found only at the end, the upstream controls are weak.

## CAPA Discipline
- Corrective action fixes the immediate cause.
- Preventive action changes the system so the issue is less likely to recur.
- Every major recurring issue should end with an owner, due date, and effectiveness check.

## Example Problem Statements
- Pick accuracy dropped in Zone A during the evening shift.
- PRE_PUTAWAY aging increased after supplier delivery peaks.
- Inventory adjustments for one SKU family doubled after a packaging change.
- Carrier delays are causing missed dispatch cutoffs on one route.

## Consultant Improvement Techniques
- Review metrics at the level where action can happen: shift, zone, supplier, customer, or SKU family.
- Do not punish teams for surfacing problems; hidden data is worse than bad data.
- Use process confirmation walks to validate that stated SOPs match floor reality.
- Keep one visible action log for recurring issues so improvement work is not lost between meetings.
